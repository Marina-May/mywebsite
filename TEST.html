Advantages and Disadvantages of Rigid Pavement

Advantages

  1. Low maintenance costs
  2. Long Life with extreme durability
  3. High value as a base for future resurfacing with asphalt
  4. Load distribution over a wide area decreasing base and sub-grade requirements
  5. Ability to place directly on poor soils
  6. No damage from oils and greases
  7. Strong edges

Disadvantages

  1. High initial costs
  2. Joint required for contraction and expansion
  3. Generally rough riding quality
  4. High repair costs

Introduction about Rigid Pavement

Rigid Pavement is the road which is made up of concrete. A rigid pavement is constructed from cement concrete or reinforced concrete slabs. Grouted concrete roads are in the category of Semi-Rigid Pavement. The design of Rigid Pavement is based on providing a structural cement concrete slab of sufficient strength to resist the loads from Traffic.

Types of Rigid Pavement

Rigid pavements are differentiated into three major categories by their means of crack control.They are

  1. Jointed Plain Concrete Pavement ( JPCP)
  2. Jointed Reinforces Concrete Pavement ( JRCP)
  3. Continuously Reinforced Concrete Pavement ( CRCP)
